I’m a designer.

37signals. Chicago. No bullshit.

I’m a designer at 37signals.

I work in Chicago, but I also enjoy working remote from my other favorite cities. Most of my time is spent crafting work at 37signals. I have a hand in everything from adding features to Basecamp, shaping the 37signals voice within our marketing, all the way to product design and prototyping. I also teach at The Starter League, and serve as a mentor with the AIGA.

Prior to 37signals, I cut my teeth three years as an interactive designer at Threadless. All the while, I’ve done work for other really great clients including Google, Harley-Davidson, Rand McNally, Sears and SC Johnson.

I’ve had the honor of being recognized with AIGA, Coudal, HOW, Rockport, Print, UnderConsideration and other fancy design publications. You can often find me speaking about this stuff at places such as PSFK, The School of Visual Arts, and Zappos.

Other interests include, though not limited to: pizza, beer and shots of whiskey.

I’m a designer at 37signals focused on crafting good things for good people.

This will be the long version.

Teaching

During my nights, I teach and mentor beginners the fundamentals of graphic design and visual patterns at The Starter League. Spending time as a teacher has shaped me into better designer, and a better communicator. Before The Starter League, I taught art direction, design, and professional practices at The Chicago Portfolio School for three years.
