I’m a designer from Chicago making and breaking the web at 37signals.

I live and work in Chicago, but I also enjoy working remote from my other favorite cities. Much of my work at 37signals is consumer facing, where projects range from improving conversion and retention of our customers, adding new features to Basecamp, shaping the 37signals voice through marketing, all the way to product design and app prototyping.

Prior to 37signals, I cut my teeth for three years as the first full-time interactive designer at Threadless. All the while, I’ve done work for clients including Google, Harley-Davidson, Rand McNally, Sears and SC Johnson.

Teaching, mentoring, and community outreach is a large part of my body of work. I started and managed the Chicago chapter of CreativeMornings for two years, and have since passed the reigns to my friends at Knoed. I serve as a design mentor within the AIGA, and various startup accelerators.

Every now and again, you can find me speaking about my experiences at places including PSFK, The School of Visual Arts, and companies like Zappos.

I’m a designer.

I make things for the web at 37signals. Formerly Threadless, and an ad agency refugee. I believe in hard work and building your community. ♥ Chicago.

About this site…

This site is designed using a flexible six-column grid from sketch directly to code. Programmed with Sublime Text 2 using Jekyll, SCSS, CoffeeScript, and Rake tasks for compilation and deployment to Dreamhost. Version controlled with Git on GitHub. Typefaces are Alto by OurType and FF Tisa served via Typekit. Photoshop and Illustrator for imagery. No frameworks, get weird.
