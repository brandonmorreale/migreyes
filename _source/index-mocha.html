I’m Mig Reyes. I’m a graphic and web designer from Chicago working at Basecamp.

Making

I spend most of my time making and marketing Basecamp, the world’s best project management software. I came over from Threadless, where I was their first fulltime interactive designer. With my roots in traditional graphic design, I like to experiment with other types of non-web and product work as well.

Doing

I volunteer a large portion of my time to community outreach. Since 2009, I’ve been teaching design and art direction within Chicago portfolio and tech schools. I also maintain Humble Pied, a video publication with the design industry’s smartest people.

Writing

I’m trying to be a better writer everyday. I like to write about design, craft, and culture. You can read some of my favorite pieces so far: Try, try again, When culture turns to policy, and Reminder: Design is still about words.

Speaking

Every now and again I travel to give presentations on design. My last favorite talk was the one I gave at CreativeMornings Chicago. It’s four pieces of backwards advice that captures what I’ve been thinking about as we live our lives as creators.

Learn more about me, or read the things I believe in.
